ね

ENね - sentence-final confirmation / agreement particle

JA「〜ね」(かくにん)

Explanation

ね at sentence-end seeks agreement or shared feeling - 'isn't it?', 'right?'. Used when the speaker assumes the listener has the same view.

Attaches to

sentence_end

Examples

JAいい てんきですね。
ENNice weather, isn't it?
JAおいしいですね。
ENIt's delicious, isn't it?
JAあの 人は しんせつですね。
ENThat person is kind, right?
JAむずかしかったですね。
ENThat was tough, wasn't it?
JAそうですね。
ENThat's right / That's so.
JAじが きれいですね。
ENYour handwriting is pretty.
JAけっこうな おちゃですね。
ENWhat a fine tea.
JAあついですね。
ENIt's hot, isn't it?
JAたかいですね。
ENIt's expensive, isn't it?
JAおもしろいですね。
ENIt's interesting, isn't it?

Common mistakes

✗ 「あした 雨が ふりますね。」(when telling someone NEW info they didn't know)
✓ 「あした 雨が ふりますよ。」(use よ for new info)
ね seeks agreement on SHARED knowledge. For information the listener doesn't have yet, use よ instead.
✗ いい てんきですよ。
✓ いい てんきですね。
ね seeks AGREEMENT about something speaker and listener both observe ('nice weather, isn't it?'). よ ASSERTS new information to listener ('it's nice weather, fyi'). For weather small-talk, ね is correct — both parties see the weather.
✗ いい てんきです。 (with falling intonation, no ね)
✓ いい てんきですね。
ね attaches AT THE END of a finished sentence — after です/ます, not before. Adding ね assumes shared observation with the listener. Dropping ね changes the sentence from agreement-seeking to bare assertion.

Wrong / corrected pairs

✗ いい てんきです ね。 (about your own observation, no agreement sought)
✓ いい てんきです。
ね seeks agreement / shared perception. If listener cannot evaluate, ね sounds odd.
✗ がくせいですね。 (asking about listener)
✓ がくせいですか。
For factual yes/no question use か. ね assumes the speaker already believes the proposition.
✗ たべますね だ。
✓ たべますね。
ね attaches at the END; do not add だ/です after.
