## THE NATIVE-HINDI-SCHOLAR QUALITY RUBRIC

A native Hindi scholar reviewing a JLPT-pedagogical Hindi gloss
applies these tests in order. Any failed test = flag for rewrite.

### R-1. Script integrity (mechanical)

- The string contains at least one Devanagari character
  (Unicode U+0900–U+097F) UNLESS the slot is documented allowlist
  (font-size labels, proper nouns, ASCII JLPT terminology like
  "JLPT" or "N5").
- No mojibake, no replacement characters (U+FFFD), no zero-width
  joiners where they don't belong.
- Half-form / half-letter consonants render correctly: ज्ञ (j-ñ)
  vs ज + ् + ञ etc. The visual presentation in the running app
  is the test.
- Nukta usage is correct: क़ / ख़ / ग़ / ज़ / फ़ / ड़ / ढ़ for the
  six relevant consonants ONLY when the source phoneme demands
  them. क़ान्जी "kanji" is wrong; कान्जी is right (Japanese
  loanword, no Persian/Arabic origin).

### R-2. Naturalness (the most important test)

- Read the Hindi sentence aloud. Would a native-Hindi-speaking
  Indian Hindi-medium learner produce this sentence
  spontaneously, or does it sound like a Google Translate output
  /a calque from English?
- Word order should be natural Hindi (SOV, postpositional). A
  literal English-to-Hindi rendering with English word order is
  a fail.
- Idiomatic verb phrases > literal verb phrases. "to study" =
  पढ़ाई करना or अध्ययन करना (context-dependent), not "अध्ययन
  to-do" calque.

### R-3. Register match

- The English source for vocab gloss is typically dictionary-
  short (1-3 words). The Hindi gloss MUST be similarly short.
  Long, explanatory Hindi for a short English gloss is wrong.
- Grammar `explanation_hi` is teaching prose: register should
  match a Hindi-medium textbook (formal-but-accessible, second-
  person तुम/आप-respectful tone). NOT casual तू / तेरा.
- L1 notes (`l1_notes.hi`) are pedagogical comparisons. Tone
  should be encouraging and concrete: "हिंदी की तरह ... जापानी
  में भी ...". Avoid jargon (linguist-grade terminology like
  "ergativity" / "perfective aspect") unless inline-explained.

### R-4. Vocabulary appropriateness for मानक हिंदी

- Prefer मानक हिंदी (Hindi medium of instruction) vocabulary.
  - Persian/Arabic-origin words (वक़्त, मुश्किल, सच्चा) are fine
    when they are colloquial defaults; avoid heavy Urdu-register
    substitutions (मुलाक़ात-ए-दिल style).
  - Sanskrit-origin words are fine for academic concepts (व्याकरण,
    अध्ययन, पठन) but avoid शुद्ध हिंदी / Sanskritized over-formal
    vocabulary for everyday concepts (e.g., do not use "मार्ग"
    where "रास्ता" is the everyday word).
- For loanwords from English with no settled Hindi equivalent,
  use Devanagari transliteration of the English word: कंप्यूटर,
  टेलीफ़ोन (note nukta on फ़), टैक्सी. This is standard मानक हिंदी
  practice.

### R-5. Grammatical accuracy

- Gender agreement is correct on every adjective, every verb in
  habitual / past forms.
  - लड़का जाता है vs लड़की जाती है vs लड़के जाते हैं — the verb
    AND adjective MUST agree.
- Postposition placement is grammatical:
  - ने (ergative) on past-perfective transitive subjects only.
  - से for "from" (source) and "by means of" (instrument) — but
    do not over-extend to where Hindi prefers other postpositions.
  - को on direct-object animates / definite-direct-objects /
    indirect-object datives.
- Compound verbs (जाना, देना, लेना as light verbs) usage is
  natural: खा लिया (consumed), खा गया (devoured), पढ़ रहा हूँ
  (am reading) — the aspect choice should match what the English
  source meant.
- Number agreement: ये किताबें हैं (plural agreement, plural
  postposition).

### R-6. JLPT-pedagogical accuracy

The Hindi explanation must be FAITHFUL to the Japanese it explains:

- A Japanese verb conjugation explanation in Hindi must not
  introduce errors about the Japanese form. The Hindi is the
  vehicle; the Japanese facts are the cargo.
- L1-interference notes must compare the *correct* Hindi form
  to the *correct* Japanese form. Comparing wrong Hindi to right
  Japanese, or right Hindi to wrong Japanese, is a hard fail —
  worse than English-only would have been.
- Any furigana / kana referenced in the Hindi explanation must
  match what the English source uses.

### R-7. Consistency across the corpus

- The same Japanese vocabulary item must have the same Hindi
  gloss across vocab.json, kanji.json (when relevant), and any
  question explanation that quotes it.
- The same grammar pattern must use the same Hindi name across
  grammar.json, the questions that reference it, and any reading
  /listening explanation that quotes it.
- Pronoun choice (तुम vs आप) MUST be consistent within a single
  surface (one passage, one explanation block) and SHOULD be
  consistent across the whole product. Default: respectful आप
  for teaching prose; second-person тुम for in-passage Japanese
  dialogue translations where the dialogue is between peers.
