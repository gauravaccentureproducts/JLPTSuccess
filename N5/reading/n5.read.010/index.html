わたしの きょうしつ

Topic: school · Mondai: 4

ENわたしの きょうしつ

HIकक्षा में 25 मेज़-कुर्सियाँ; शिक्षक की मेज़ बड़ी; खिड़की से पार्क दिखता है — सुंदर कक्षा।

Passage

私の きょうしつには つくえが 25こ あります。いすも 25こ あります。先生の つくえは おおきいです。まどから こうえんが 見えます。きれいな きょうしつです。

Translation (natural)

Our classroom has 25 desks and 25 chairs. The teacher's desk is large. You can see the park through the window — it's a really pleasant classroom.

Translation (literal)

In my classroom, there are 25 desks. There are 25 chairs as well. The teacher's desk is big. From the window, you can see the park. It is a beautiful classroom.

Cultural context

Japanese classrooms are typically arranged in 5-row × 5-column grids (≈25-40 students per class). The teacher's desk at the front + students facing forward is universal — group-work rearrangements happen but the default layout is rows. The 25-desk count in the passage is a small class by Japanese urban-school standards (40 is more common).
