まいにちの せいかつ

Topic: daily routine · Mondai: 4

ENまいにちの せいかつ

HIलेखक की दिनचर्या — सुबह 6 बजे उठना, नाश्ता, 9-5 की नौकरी, घर लौटकर टीवी, रात 10 बजे सोना।

Passage

私は 毎日 6時に おきます。あさごはんを 食べてから、しごとに 行きます。しごとは 9時から 5時までです。うちに かえってから、テレビを 見ます。10時に ねます。

Translation (natural)

I get up at 6 every day, eat breakfast, then head to work. I work from 9 to 5. After getting home I watch TV, and I'm in bed by 10.

Translation (literal)

I, every day, at 6 o'clock, get up. After eating breakfast, I go to work. Work is from 9 o'clock until 5 o'clock. After returning home, I watch TV. At 10 o'clock, I sleep.

Cultural context

A 9-to-5 workday in Japan often stretches longer in practice (残業, zangyō, "overtime" is common in white-collar roles), but the polite default written form remains "9 to 5" because overtime is socially expected, not contractually scheduled. Reading "wakes at 6, works 9-5" maps to a typical urban commuting routine.
