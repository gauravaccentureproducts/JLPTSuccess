びょうきです

Topic: health · Mondai: 4

ENびょうきです

HIकल से सिरदर्द और हल्की खाँसी; आज स्कूल छोड़कर आराम; कल डॉक्टर के पास जाना है।

Passage

きのうから あたまが いたいです。せきも すこし 出ます。今日は 学校を 休んで、うちで ねます。あした、いしゃに 行きます。はやく げんきに なりたいです。

Translation (natural)

I've had a headache since yesterday and a slight cough. I'm staying home from school today and resting in bed. I'll see the doctor tomorrow. I want to get better fast.

Translation (literal)

Since yesterday, my head has been hurting. I also have a slight cough. Today, taking the day off school, I will sleep at home. Tomorrow, I will go to the doctor. I want to get better quickly.

Cultural context

病院 (byouin) in Japanese means both "hospital" and "doctor's clinic" — the same word covers a small neighbourhood clinic and a large hospital. For a cold or headache, you visit a small 内科 (naika, internal medicine clinic) and pay a 30% out-of-pocket fee under the National Health Insurance system; the rest is covered. Same-day walk-ins are standard at clinics. Pharmacies (薬局, yakkyoku) dispense prescriptions next door.
